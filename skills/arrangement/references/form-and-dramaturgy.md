# Form, phrase architecture, and dramaturgy

> Adapted from `jazz-composition-designer` (archived portable skill package,
> consolidated 2026-07-13). Read this alongside `ideation-theory.md` §4b–4c
> and §7: that file gives the *dramatic arc* (the emotional journey that
> decides everything) and a compact structure vocabulary; this file goes
> deeper on the mechanics of form itself — bar counts, phrase length,
> section-contrast dimensions, cadence strength, solo form, and intro/ending
> strategy. Use the arc from `ideation-theory.md` to choose *which* formal
> family and phrase design serve the piece, not the reverse.

## 1. Form is recurrence plus contrast

Letters such as AABA describe related or contrasting material, not genre. Define exact bar counts, repeats, first/second endings, and transitions.

## 2. Common formal families

### Blues

- 12 bars is common, but 8-, 16-, and expanded blues forms exist.
- Structural landmarks often occur at bars 1, 5, 9, and the turnaround.

### 32-bar song forms

- AABA: statement, restatement, bridge, return.
- ABAC: repeated opening with two different continuations.
- AABC, ABAB, and variants are valid.

Do not force every standard-like composition into 32 bars.

### 16- and 24-bar forms

Useful for concise themes, modal pieces, and modern standards. Maintain phrase clarity.

### Verse-chorus and song forms

Appropriate for vocal, smooth, contemporary, jazz-pop, and hybrid compositions. Distinguish pop `chorus` from jazz `one chorus of the form`. Assign labels by audible function rather than position, and read `song-architecture.md` for hook-first openings, verses, pre-choruses, refrains, post-choruses, bridges, solo integration, tags, codas, and outros.

### Vamp/riff forms

Organize through texture, layering, solo entries, and cue-based transitions rather than many chord changes.

### Through-composed and episodic

Use for chamber jazz, third stream, fusion suites, narrative works, and complex meters. Provide recognizable anchors or processes.

### Open/cue-based forms

Define event order, cue authority, duration ranges, and return points.

## 3. Phrase length

Use regular or irregular phrases intentionally:

- 4+4;
- 2+2+4;
- 3+5;
- 5+5+6;
- elision where one phrase ending begins the next;
- extension or truncation at climactic points.

Irregularity should be audible as design, not accidental bar-count error.

## 4. Section contrast

Create contrast through selected dimensions:

- tonal center or mode;
- harmonic rhythm;
- bass behavior;
- meter or subdivision;
- motif register/contour;
- orchestration;
- density and dynamics;
- articulation;
- fixed versus open material.

Retain one invariant to preserve identity.

## 5. Formal harmonic strategy

Assign phrase-end strength:

- weak continuation;
- half cadence/suspension;
- local arrival;
- global cadence;
- deceptive extension;
- open-ended cue.

Do not give every four bars the same cadence.

## 6. Solo form

Choose among:

- same form as head;
- simplified changes;
- separate vamp;
- alternate open section;
- backgrounds entering on later choruses;
- form shortened/expanded by cue.

State how players track the form and re-enter the head.

## 7. Intro strategies

- composed thematic fragment;
- vamp;
- last four/eight bars of form;
- rubato cadenza;
- drum/bass groove reveal;
- free texture resolving into countable time;
- chorale or counterpoint.

The intro should establish or strategically withhold key, tempo, motif, or groove.

## 8. Ending strategies

- final cadence and held chord;
- tag of last two/four bars;
- vamp with cue;
- stop ending;
- ritardando/rubato coda;
- reharmonized final phrase;
- fade concept for production, with live alternative;
- open cutoff cue.

Write exact cue and bar count.

## 9. Dramatic arc

Map across the performance:

- dynamics;
- register;
- density;
- harmonic tension;
- rhythmic subdivision;
- orchestration;
- improvisational freedom.

A typical arc may build, drop, and rebuild, but other shapes are valid. Ensure the final head does not feel mechanically pasted after solos.

## 10. Duration planning

Calculate approximate performance length from:

- BPM and beats per bar;
- bars per form;
- head in/out;
- solo choruses;
- open sections;
- intros/interludes/coda;
- expected rubato.

State whether the estimate assumes no repeats or performance repeats.

## 11. Form audit

- Do bar counts add up?
- Are repeats/endings unambiguous?
- Does each labeled song section perform the function implied by its name?
- Is the hook distinct from the chorus or refrain when the design requires that distinction?
- Does the bridge or contrasting section earn its role?
- Is the solo architecture compatible with the head?
- Are transitions cued?
- Does the performance arc have a clear beginning, development, and ending?

## 11a. Dramatic arc, tension-release, dan song structure (dari `ideation-theory.md` §4b/§4c/§7)

Dipindah dari `melody-fundamentals.md` (rapikan Task 3 — bukan materi
melodi; disatukan di sini karena melengkapi §9 Dramatic arc dan §2 Common
formal families di atas).

**Dramatic arc — tulang punggung.** Progresi chord yang berulang tanpa
bentuk emosi adalah loop, bukan karya. Arc adalah perjalanan emosi lagu
dari satu rasa ke rasa lain, dan ini keputusan induk yang melayani semua
keputusan lain. Nyatakan arc sebagai urutan rasa per section, mis. "ragu →
harap → puncak → penerimaan" atau "tenang → rindu → hangat → istirahat".
Tiap rasa menuntut kondisi musikal spesifik:

- **fase mencari/ragu** — register rendah-menengah, melodi berhenti di
  nada belum resolve (2, 6, 7), instrumentasi jarang, timing santai;
- **fase membangun/harap** — ritme mengencang, bass melangkah naik,
  register merangkak naik, instrumen masuk satu-satu, motif diulang lebih
  tinggi (sequence);
- **fase puncak** — register tinggi, ensemble penuh, melodi mencapai dan
  *menahan* nada tertingginya, harmoni terbuka/mayor, on-the-beat (percaya
  diri);
- **fase resolusi** — register turun, ritme melonggar, resolusi penuh ke
  tonic, ruang dan keheningan, ambience melebar.

Arc menentukan sepi-nya verse, cara pre-chorus membangun, apakah chorus
membuka atau meledak, dan bagaimana ending mendarat. Desain ini sebelum
chord. Kalau brief hanya berupa vibe, usulkan arc dan konfirmasi.

**Tension-release sebagai dramaturgi** (bagian dari §9 di atas, bukan
mekanik interval melodi — lihat `melody-fundamentals.md` untuk sisi
phrasing-nya): bangun tensi lalu lepaskan, tapi pilih hanya teknik yang
melayani arc saat itu — menumpuk semuanya adalah over-arrangement.
Paletnya: dominant chord ditahan sebelum resolve ke tonic; bass bergerak
berlawanan arah dengan melodi (friksi); ritme mengencang (subdivisi makin
rapat); instrumen ditambah satu-satu; register naik; volume/intensitas
naik; reverb/ambience melebar; satu ketuk hening tepat sebelum section
besar (membuat entrance terasa kena); broken chord membuka ke ruang; dan
keheningan/ruang yang disengaja sebagai release itu sendiri. Section yang
hanya membangun tanpa pernah lepas jadi melelahkan; yang hanya melepas
tanpa membangun jadi hanyut — arc menentukan mana yang dibutuhkan tiap
section.

**Song structure (pelengkap keluarga bentuk jazz di §2):**

- Bentuk jazz: **AABA (32-bar)**, **blues (12-bar)**, **ABAC**, atau
  **through-composed** (lihat §2 untuk detail). Bentuk berbasis loop:
  **intro → loop A → variasi → kontras (B) → transformed return → outro**.
- Tiap section berulang harus *bervariasi* — reorkestrasi, reharmonisasi,
  fragmentasi, penipisan tekstur, atau transformasi. Repetisi literal tanpa
  tujuan adalah penyebab #1 kebosanan.
- Rencanakan **macro arc**: di mana klimaks, di mana subtraction/breakdown,
  di mana ending. Bahkan loop lofi yang santai tetap butuh satu build dan
  satu drop.
- Beri lagu **ending yang jelas** — tag, ritard, chord final, atau titik
  loop yang bersih. Jangan biarkan loop tanpa keputusan keluar.
