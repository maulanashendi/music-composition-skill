# Interaction map — "Settling In (Late)"

Per `references/interaction-map.md`: for each section, is each instrument
**lead / support / answer / silent**? The map translates the plan's arc
(doubt -> turning -> acceptance) into who plays when. This is the map that
was actually realized in `song.abc` + `drums.json` (not a separate,
aspirational document) -- every row below traces to specific bars in those
two files.

## Reading the map against the arc

- **Doubt (Intro/A1, bars 1-12):** one or two voices active at a time, the
  Rhodes is the only "leader" until bar 5 (Module 4 Lens 2: Intro is led by
  texture/comping, not melody -- exactly as `reasoning-theory.md` prescribes).
  Drums stay minimal throughout and thin *further* at the section's close
  (bar 12), rather than building -- doubt does not resolve into hope here.
- **Turning (B, bars 13-16):** exactly one dimension lifts at a time
  (register + one borrowed chord + hat activity); the comping voicing family
  and groove pattern shape stay anchored so the lift reads as "the same piece,
  slightly brighter" rather than a new, more energetic song. No tutti hit is
  used anywhere in this section -- deliberately, because the brief forbids a
  big triumphant swell.
- **Acceptance (Outro, bars 17-24):** subtraction is the *only* device used to
  carry the section (kick removed first, then the lead goes silent for two
  bars, then drums go fully silent for the final bar). Nothing new enters;
  everything present is something the listener has already heard, now
  thinning out. This is what makes the ending read as settling rather than
  arriving somewhere new.

## Quality-gate check (per `interaction-map.md`'s own checklist)

- Does each section's density match its arc phase? Yes -- doubt is sparsest
  at its very end (bar 12), turning is the single densest few bars but still
  well short of a "peak" (no tutti, no full drum kit, register capped), and
  acceptance strictly decreases in density bar by bar.
- Is any instrument active only to fill space? No -- every "support" role
  above ties to a specific function (comping the harmony, carrying the root,
  keeping time); nothing is present just to avoid a bar looking sparse in the
  file.
- Is there at least one subtraction and one call/lead handoff? Yes -- the
  Outro's kick-then-lead-then-drums subtraction sequence, and the
  Rhodes-states-the-hook-before-the-lead-does handoff in the Intro.
- Would a listener feel the journey? This is the one question this document
  cannot answer for itself (per `reasoning-theory.md` Module 7: "what the
  agent cannot do" -- judging whether it is actually felt is a human,
  ear-first call). The map is built to make that felt journey *possible*,
  not to certify that it lands.
