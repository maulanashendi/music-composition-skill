# 07 — Comping dan Voicing (Level 7, Rhodes)

Comping chart per bagian:

- **Intro**: tacet.
- **A (3-6)**: sparse, 2 attacks/bar, rootless shell voicings
  (e.g. Dm11 -> F-A-C-E in the right hand, no root — bass has the root),
  placed in the melody's silent "4" group so comping and melody don't
  collide rhythmically.
- **A′ (7-10)**: syncopated responses — comping now answers the sax
  motif's tail (call and response) rather than just marking the harmony;
  voicing follows the outside chord at bar 8 (Ebmaj7#11 rootless:
  G-Bb-D-Ab) with the same voice-leading logic as the inside Dm9 shape
  (top note steps: A(9 of Dm9) -> Ab(#11 of Eb) -> A(back), a half-step
  wobble that mirrors the melody's side-slip).
- **B (11-14)**: denser voicing (spread, adding the color tone each bar:
  #11/#9/b13 as appropriate), rhythmically busier, tracking the rising
  sequence bar to bar — comping is now driving the tension, not just
  filling.
- **Climax (15-18)**: simplified back to strong sustained/hit voicings in
  unison rhythm with sax and drums — density in *dynamics*, not in note
  count; this is deliberate contrast with B's business (see
  `RED-FLAGS.md` on stacking every device — climax reads bigger by being
  rhythmically unified, not by being the busiest bar).
- **Coda (19-20)**: sustained Dmaj9#11 voicing held through the final hit.

Voice leading check: top note across A/A′ moves F -> C -> F -> F# -> E ->
Ab -> E -> Bb — mostly stepwise/half-step motion between adjacent bars
(the two leaps, F->C in bar3->4 and E->Ab in bar8, both immediately
recover stepwise per the "leap -> recovery" principle from Level 4).
