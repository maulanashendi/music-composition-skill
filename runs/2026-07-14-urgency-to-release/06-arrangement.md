# 06 — Aransemen Instrumen (Level 6)

Orchestration map (density: sparse -> medium -> dense -> full -> sparse):

- **Intro (1-2)**: bass + drums only (kick/hat), establishing the 4+3
  riff. Sax and Rhodes silent — the piece opens lonely/restless before
  the identity (motif) is even heard.
- **A (3-6)**: sax carries the head motif; Rhodes plays sparse hits
  (2 per bar, shell voicings) in the "4" group the melody leaves empty;
  bass+drums continue the reference groove.
- **A′ (7-10)**: Rhodes comping becomes more active/syncopated (call and
  response with the sax motif); bass starts chromatic-approach motion
  under bar 8's outside chord; drums add snare fills every 2 bars.
- **B (11-14)**: full rhythm section active and building; Rhodes plays
  denser voicings tracking the rising sequence; drums intensify
  hi-hat/ride subdivision + fills; bass locks the reference layer harder
  as harmony gets more outside (a stable low end is what keeps bars 11-14
  legible instead of just "noisy").
- **Climax (15-18)**: full quartet, sax and Rhodes in unison/harmonized
  hits on the brightened D triumphant material; drums fullest (open
  hi-hat/crash, doubled hits).
- **Coda (19-20)**: sudden drop to sax + Rhodes sustained chord + one
  final unison hit; drums single accent then silence.

Density curve: sparse (intro) -> medium (A) -> medium-dense (A′) ->
dense/building (B) -> full (climax) -> sparse (coda). No section has all
four instruments at full activity simultaneously except the climax and
the very final hit — reserved so the climax actually reads as a peak.
