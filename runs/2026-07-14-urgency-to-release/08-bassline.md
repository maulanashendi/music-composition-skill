# 08 — Bass Line (Level 8)

Approach: **ostinato/riff-based ("broken groove")** for the modal/vamp
bars, shifting to **chromatic-approach/counterline** motion under the
outside-material bars — matching `05-groove.md`'s note that bass is the
"reference layer" that keeps the meter legible while harmony gets outside.

Per section:

- **Intro (1-2)**: states the riff alone — D (root) on the "4" group's
  downbeat, then a syncopated D-A-D figure in the "3" group, establishing
  4+3 before anyone else enters.
- **A (3-6)**: root-anchored riff following the Dm11/G13sus/Cmaj7#11
  changes (D...-G...-D...-C..., always attacking the downbeat of the "4"
  group then a passing tone into the "3" group) — contour, not static
  root-holding: D-F-D (bar3), G-B-D (bar4, chord tones not just root),
  D-F-A (bar5), C-E-G (bar6).
- **A′ (7-10)**: same riff shape under Dm9, then **chromatic approach**
  into bar 8's Ebmaj7#11 (D -> Eb directly, half-step side-slip mirroring
  the melody), back to Dm9 shape bar 9, then walking approach into bar
  10's G7alt (D-F-A-Ab, Ab as chromatic approach/tritone color to G).
- **B (11-14)**: counterline motion — bass no longer just outlines the
  chord root but walks a rising line under the sequence (Bb-D-F / Ab-C-Eb
  / G-B-D / A-C#-E), reinforcing the same rising sequence the melody and
  comping carry, so all three layers push toward bar 15 together.
- **Climax (15-18)**: pedal point on D under the brightened harmony (the
  "same root, brighter mode" mechanism from `03-harmony.md`) with octave
  jumps for triumphant weight, not stepwise motion — the release is felt
  as much in the bass register/leap as in the chord quality.
- **Coda (19-20)**: sustained low D, final unison hit landing on D again.
