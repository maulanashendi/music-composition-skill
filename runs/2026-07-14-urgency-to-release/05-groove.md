# 05 — Ritme dan Groove (Level 5), + feed ke Level 8 (bass) dan Level 9 (drum)

Feel: **odd meter, 7/8 grouped 4+3** (long-short), straight (not swung)
eighth-note subdivision — fusion idiom, not swing.
Rhythmic identity: syncopated/anticipatory. The core motif (see
`04-melody.abc`) deliberately sits in the short "3" group so the meter's
asymmetry is audible in the melody, not just implied by the bass/drums.

Reference layer (per `groove-rhythm/references/groove-meter.md` §15 —
"keep at least one layer clear"): **bass and kick lock the 4+3 grouping
every bar without exception**; melody, comping, and snare/ride are allowed
to syncopate against it, but the bass/kick pairing is the thing a listener
can always re-find the "1" from.

## Bass motion (feeds 08-bassline.md)

Root-anchored riff in bars where harmony is a modal/vamp chord (Dm11,
Dmaj9#11), moving to guide-tone/chromatic-approach motion under the
outside-material bars (8, 11-14) so the bass itself tracks the tension
build, not just the chord symbols above it.

## Drum character (feeds 09-drums.json)

Intro: kick+hat only, spare, establishing 4+3.
A/A′: ride-driven, syncopated snare hits accenting the "3" group's
first eighth (where the melody motif also lands) — reference layer stays
locked underneath.
B: busier hi-hat/ride subdivision, snare fills growing every 2 bars,
building toward the climax.
Climax: full kit, open hi-hat/crash on the downbeat of bar 15, hits
doubling the ensemble's unison figure.
Coda: single final accent, then cut to near-silence.

## Gate check (Level 5)

Feel (odd-meter 7/8, 4+3, straight eighths) and rhythmic identity
(syncopated/anticipatory with a locked bass/kick reference layer)
determined explicitly.
