# 12 — Intro dan Ending (Level 12)

## Intro (bars 1-2)

Choice: **bass ostinato + drum groove** (not rubato/pedal-point-only) —
introduces the 4+3 asymmetry as a physical, rhythmic fact before any
pitched melody exists, which fits "restless/unsettled" better than a
rubato wash would. The bass riff previewed here (D-...-D-A-D, see
`08-bassline.md`) is the same shape the bass plays under the head in A,
so the intro is a genuine preview of the piece's identity, not a
placeholder vamp.

## Ending (bars 19-20)

Choice: **final unison hit + short tag**, not a fade or unresolved
dominant — brief explicitly wants a "triumphant, released climax," and an
unresolved or faded ending would undercut that release right after
achieving it. Bar 19 restates the climax's brightened Dmaj9#11 with the
full-ensemble unison hit (echoing the motif's rising-4th shape one last
time, widened to octaves — see `04-melody.abc` Tahap 9), bar 20 resolves
to a plain open D, drums dropping to a single accent then silence. The
ending is tied to the main motif (same interval identity, just widened),
not an unrelated cadential formula bolted on.

## Gate check (Level 12)

Intro and ending both have identity tied to the piece's own material
(bass riff / head motif), not generic placeholders.
