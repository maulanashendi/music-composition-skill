# Level 2 — Arsitektur Lagu: kandidat & seleksi

## Objective
Bentuk keseluruhan yang mewujudkan arc "sepi-gelap → tumbuh bertahap → hangat-hidup" dari Level 1, dengan satu micro-apex, dalam 20-28 bar.

## Immutable constraints
- Bass ostinato dua-nada dipertahankan hampir sepanjang piece (Level 1).
- Layer masuk satu-satu: bass → drum ringan → Rhodes pad → sax lead → call-response lokal → tekstur hangat penuh.
- Ending rekontekstualisasi (nada tegang sama, harmoni baru).
- Panjang 20-28 bar.

## Assumptions
- 24 bar dipakai sebagai titik tengah rentang; dikonfirmasi ulang di kedua kandidat di bawah (keduanya 24 bar, dibagi 6 section x 4 bar untuk selaras dengan tahap ignition-per-layer Level 1).

## Kandidat

### Kandidat 1 — Linear (through-composed murni)
Intro(4) → Layer-Drum(4) → Layer-Rhodes(4) → Layer-Sax(4) → Call-Response/micro-apex(4) → Coda-rekontekstualisasi(4) = 24 bar.
Setiap section menambah SATU layer baru saja, tidak pernah mundur. Micro-apex ditempatkan di section ke-5 (call-response), tepat sebelum coda.
Risiko: bentuk sepenuhnya monoton-menaik bisa terasa "prediktif" — begitu didengar polanya (tiap 4 bar nambah 1 layer), pendengar bisa menebak sisa lagu tanpa kejutan.

### Kandidat 2 — Return-with-dip ("subuh yang ragu")
Intro(4) → Layer-Drum(4) → Layer-Rhodes(4) → **Dip** — kembali ke tekstur bass+drum minimal saja selama 2 bar seperti "kota sempat diam lagi sebelum benar-benar bangun" (2) → Layer-Sax+Call-Response/micro-apex(6) → Coda-rekontekstualisasi(4) = 24 bar.
Dip di bar 15-16 memberi non-linearitas kecil sebelum dorongan akhir ke micro-apex — meniru realita pasar subuh yang tidak bangun mulus (ada jeda sunyi sesaat sebelum benar-benar ramai).
Risiko: dip bisa terasa seperti "mundur"/kontradiksi arc "bertahap tumbuh" kalau tidak di-setup jelas sebagai bagian dari realisme arc, bukan kesalahan pacing — perlu transisi 3-fase (Level 11) yang eksplisit supaya dip terdengar disengaja, bukan energi yang jatuh tanpa alasan.

## Selected + alasan

**Dipilih: Kandidat 2 — Return-with-dip ("subuh yang ragu")** (verdict selector segar):

> The brief explicitly asks for gradual, non-monotonic waking with exactly one micro-apex, not a climax. Candidate 1's every-4-bars-plus-one-layer pattern is audible as a pattern almost immediately (by bar 8), which flattens the micro-apex into just "the next scheduled increment" — nothing before it dipped or held back, so there's no contrast to make it register as special. Candidate 2's 2-bar dip gives the ear a moment of "wait, did it pull back?" right before the push — the market waking in fits and starts rather than executing a checklist. That contrast is what makes the subsequent micro-apex land as an apex at all.
>
> Execution requirement to neutralize the "reads as backtracking" risk: keep the dip texturally honest (bass+drum only, no new harmonic material, no melodic idea abandoned mid-phrase) so it reads as "held breath" not "lost momentum"; land back into the sax/call-response section on a rhythmic pickup, not a bar-one downbeat.
>
> **Graft from Candidate 1:** protect the pre-dip stretch (intro → drum → Rhodes) as strictly one-layer-at-a-time, no fussiness — Candidate 1's cleanest asset.
